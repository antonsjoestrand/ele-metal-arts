Harley Eagle

We will proudly present the Harley Eagle on the floor of our dealership for years to come. A second sculpture for another one of our dealerships is already in the works, and we have no doubt that Elaine will blow us away again!

Feathered Panlong

I came to Elaine with a very special request that was close to my heart, to recreate my vision for the Imperial Water Dragon. And wow did she manage to do that! She told me that it was going to be a challenge and I completely understood that. But I never expected her to exceed my expectations in every way imaginable. The only way to describe it is if someone looked into your mind and painted an accurate picture of what you were imagining, and then made it ten times better. The dragon now rests in Central Park and everyone I've seen walk by have just been amazed by it!

- Will Borrow

Power and Authority

Everything about this project is just perfection. The name, the style, the detail, everything. Elaine told me about her interest for eagles and I knew immediately that she was the right one for the job. We spent a lot of time deciding on positioning and smaller details like how much the eagle's beak was going to be open. And that is really what makes Elaine stand out from the rest, she cares about every single detail you could possibly imagine. The end result blew my mind more than I can even describe. It is proudly displayed in the heart of downtown Los Angeles, and people have told me time and time again how beautiful it is. I will keep Elaine in mind for all of our future projects and artwork we might need.

- Justin Case

The Totem

It is safe to say that we quickly realized that Elaine was perfect for this project when she told us about how she grew up watching native carvers and pole builders. The combination of that and her incredible artistic style is truly what brought this totem to life. We loved it so much that we asked her to create a second smaller sculpture for our park. We call it "The Mystical Owl" and Elaine once again did a fantastic job! People come to the park just to see these two sculptures, which I think is pretty amazing!

- Lance Borgal
