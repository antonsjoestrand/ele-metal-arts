My Projects

Harley Eagle

Vancouver

I created a mixed medium metal eagle using stainless steel and bronze to create a similar sculpture to the larger eagle I built for Los Angeles in 2018. With an attacking pose, stainless steel head and tail feathers this eagle has a 12-feet wingspan and a metal base so it could be displayed on the floor. This majestic eagle is now displayed at the Harley-Davidson dealership in Vancouver.

Feathered Panlong

New York

Creating my vision of the "Imperial Water Dragon" was an artistic challenge and accomplishment that is a personal highlight of my career as an artist. To be able to learn from other cultures and incorporate the belief, folklore and design style into my own artistic creation and share my creation within the culture is an honor as an artist and human being. The Feathered Panlong is displayed in Central Park, New York.

Power and Authority

Los Angeles

This eagle is a lot bigger than it looks. Power and Authority is a 20-feet high sculpture with a 31-feet wingspan that I completed in 2018. I hand fabricated the surgical grade stainless steel and polished it with a mirror finish. The beauty and detail of this piece is unparalleled, and is realistic in detail from every angle. Today it is displayed in downtown Los Angeles.

The Totem

Chicago

My personal feelings about The Totem run deep, having been born and raised on the West Coast and Vancouver Island. I grew up surrounded by native culture and have known many great native carvers and pole builders over the years. Trying to stay with traditional styling I spent several months completing the totem you see here. Standing 12-feet tall the pole represents a souls journey through life. You can find this piece in Burnham Park, Chicago.
